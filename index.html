About us


Our speech and debate camp will be geared towards students in 3rd to 7th grade.

Each student will learn skills in public speaking, critical thinking, and research.

Each session will be one hour, with optional office hours after, and will occur three times a week: Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. This camp will run from July 1st to 26th. Depending on the availability of the kids, camp sessions will either be in the morning (10:30 AM - 11:30 AM) or in the afternoon (4:30 PM - 5:30 PM). Optional office hours can be scheduled by the parent or kid and will take place for thirty minutes after the camp session.

In order to ensure the commitment of the students, we are charging a $40.00 fee. If the student is present at every class (running from July 1st to July 26th), this fee will be refunded. Parents must notify coaches ahead of time for exceptions.

Curriculum


Week Day Session Focus
One: Platform and Interp Monday Meet instructors and students, play games, intro to interp (speech acting), use of voice/gestures, student script presentations with feedback
One Wednesday Intro to platform speeches, vocal exercises, icebreakers, watch champion clips, work on speech about favorite movie, Q&A session.
One Friday Focus on delivery improvement, fun games, vocal exercises, speech writing, 3-5 minute speech performances with peer feedback.
Two: Spontaneous Monday Intro to spontaneous speeches, quick-thinking games, bonding activities, collaborative spontaneous speech performance.
Two Wednesday Start giving speeches on the spot with minimal prep time, game to improve speaking skills.
Two Friday Structured speeches, 2 minute speeches with 5 minutes prep time, option to challenge themselves further.
Three: Debate Monday Introduction to debating, topic introduction, brainstorming arguments.
Three Wednesday Debate terminologies, successful debate examples (actors, lawyers, presidents), fundamentals of debate, intro to SPAR (spontaneous argumentation), prepare arguments for practice debate.
Three Friday Practice debates, preparation for internal tournament.
Four: Internal Tournament Monday Showcase skills in a fun competition, decide event for competition, end with fun exercise.
Four Wednesday Speech competition day, compete with own script or speech on societal issue, rankings disclosed, certificates for all

Four Friday Debate tournament, compete against each other, certificate distribution.




FAQ


What if I don't have experience?

That's completely fine! This camp is completely beginner friendly. That being said, it's also useful to those looking to advance their speech and debate skills.

Do I have to participate in the tournament at the end?

No, that is optional. However, we strongly reccomend participating as there is no better way to gain tournament experience than by competing with your peers.

What will students be able to accomplish in this course?

After these four weeks, students will have enough experience to know what events they want to compete in and sign up for tournaments. Of course, there is still much more they could learn but there is no better way to start that with Summit Speech and Debate.

What should I do after taking this course?

After taking this course, students should consider signing up for a more advanced year round course (which we may offer depending on interest). They should also consider competing in tournaments as that is the best way to keep on improving.
